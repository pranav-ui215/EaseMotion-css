CSS-only motion

Ease Accordion

Expand panels to their natural height with modern CSS, while keeping a graceful grid fallback for browsers that do not animate to auto yet.

The preferred path uses interpolate-size: allow-keywords and transitions from height: 0 to height: auto. This avoids the uneven easing caused by arbitrary max-height values.

Panels can contain short copy, longer paragraphs, lists, or inline controls. The transition is based on the rendered content instead of a fixed numeric ceiling.

  • Works with variable copy length.
  • Uses no JavaScript.
  • Keeps overflow clipped during the animation.

Browsers without auto-size interpolation use the grid row fallback: the wrapper animates from 0fr to 1fr, and the inner content stays clipped until the panel finishes opening.
